# TenderFlow AI Tutorial Template

This template provides a structure for writing clear, helpful tutorials for UK care providers.

## Template Structure

```markdown
# {{feature_name}}

## 1. What This Feature Does

**Write 2-3 sentences explaining what the feature does in plain English.**

Example:
"The Dashboard is your command centre for managing tenders. It shows you at a glance how many tenders you're tracking, which ones need urgent attention, and what's happening across your organisation."

## 2. Why It Matters

**Explain the business value in 2-3 sentences. Focus on saving time, winning more bids, or reducing stress.**

Example:
"Without a dashboard, you'd need to check multiple places to see what's happening. The Dashboard saves you time by bringing everything into one place, so you can focus on winning tenders rather than chasing information."

## 3. What You Need Before You Start

**List any prerequisites. Keep it simple.**

Example:
- A TenderFlow AI account (sign up at app.tenderflow.ai)
- Your organisation set up (done during signup)
- At least one tender added (or you can add one now)

## 4. Step-by-Step Setup

**Break down the setup into numbered steps. Use clear, direct language.**

Format:
### Step 1: [Action]
[Detailed instructions]
[What you should see]

Example:
### Step 1: Navigate to the Dashboard
1. Log into TenderFlow AI
2. Click "Dashboard" in the left sidebar
3. You should see your main dashboard screen with four boxes at the top

**Screenshot:** dashboard-overview.png

### Step 2: [Next action]
[Continue with clear steps...]

## 5. How to Use It Day to Day

**Explain the typical daily/weekly workflow.**

Example:
"Most bid managers check their Dashboard first thing each morning. Here's a typical workflow:

1. **Morning check (5 minutes)**
   - Log in and review your Dashboard
   - Check 'Deadlines Approaching' for urgent items
   - Review 'High-Fit Tenders' for new opportunities

2. **Weekly review (15 minutes)**
   - Click through to each high-scoring tender
   - Assign tasks to team members
   - Update bid decisions (Bid/No Bid)

3. **When new tenders arrive**
   - Dashboard updates automatically
   - Check your Inbox for new alerts

## 6. What Happens After Setup

**Describe what users should expect next.**

Example:
"Once your Dashboard is set up:
- It updates automatically when tenders are added or changed
- High-fit tenders appear in the 'High-Fit Tenders' section
- Your KPI numbers update in real-time
- You can click any tender to see full details

## 7. Common Mistakes to Avoid

**List 3-5 common errors users make.**

Format:
- ❌ **Mistake**: [What they do wrong]
  ✅ **Instead**: [What they should do]

Example:
- ❌ **Ignoring the AI score**: Some users ignore tenders with low scores
  ✅ **Instead**: Review the score breakdown to understand why it's low - you might still want to bid

- ❌ **Not checking daily**: Checking once a week means you miss urgent deadlines
  ✅ **Instead**: Make the Dashboard your homepage and check it every morning

## 8. Troubleshooting

**Address 4-6 common problems with clear solutions.**

Format:
### Problem: [Issue description]
**Symptoms**: [What the user sees]
**Solution**: [Step-by-step fix]

Example:
### Problem: Dashboard shows no data
**Symptoms**: All your KPI boxes show zero
**Solution**: 
1. Check you're logged into the correct organisation
2. Click "Add Tender" to add your first tender
3. If you have tenders but see no data, refresh your browser (Ctrl+R or Cmd+R)
4. Still stuck? Contact support - there may be a technical issue

### Problem: [Next issue]
[Continue with solutions...]

## 9. Best Practice Tips

**Share 5-8 tips from experienced users.**

Format:
- 💡 **[Tip title]**: [Explanation]

Example:
- 💡 **Start your day here**: Make the Dashboard your browser homepage so you see it first thing
- 💡 **Set up email alerts**: Enable notifications for high-fit tenders so you never miss opportunities
- 💡 **Review weekly**: Spend 10 minutes each Monday reviewing your pipeline
- 💡 **Use filters**: Filter by deadline to focus on urgent tenders
- 💡 **Track trends**: Watch your win rate over time to see what's working

**Walk through a complete, realistic scenario.**

Format:

## Writing Guidelines

### Tone and Style
- ✅ **DO**: Write like you're explaining to a colleague over coffee
- ✅ **DO**: Use "you" and "your" (not "the user")
- ✅ **DO**: Use UK English spelling (colour, organisation, realise)
- ✅ **DO**: Use simple, everyday words
- ❌ **DON'T**: Use technical jargon without explanation
- ❌ **DON'T**: Assume technical knowledge
- ❌ **DON'T**: Be condescending or patronising

### Structure
- Keep sentences short (under 20 words when possible)
- Use bullet points and numbered lists
- Break up text with headings
- Use examples liberally
- Include screenshots (even if placeholders for now)

### Language
- "Click" not "navigate to"
- "Type" not "input"
- "Choose" not "select"
- "See" not "observe"
- Explain acronyms: "CQC (Care Quality Commission)"

### Troubleshooting
- Start with the symptom (what they see)
- Provide clear, numbered steps
- Offer an escape route ("Still stuck? Contact support")
- Acknowledge when something is difficult

### Tips
- Focus on time-saving
- Share real user insights
- Be specific ("Check every Monday at 9am" not "Check regularly")
- Link to related features

## Feature-Specific Notes

When writing about specific features:

### For Integrations (Gmail, APIs)
- Explain what OAuth means in simple terms
- Show exactly where to find credentials
- Include screenshots of external sites (Google Cloud Console, etc.)
- Provide fallback options if setup fails

### For AI Features (Scoring, Chat)
- Explain what the AI is doing (in simple terms)
- Set realistic expectations ("usually accurate but not perfect")
- Show how to override AI decisions
- Explain how to improve AI accuracy over time

### For Data Entry Features
- Provide example data users can copy/paste
- Show what good entries look like
- Warn about common data format issues
- Explain where the data appears later

### For Collaboration Features
- Show team workflows (not just individual)
- Explain notification settings
- Cover permission/role scenarios
- Demonstrate @mentions and commenting

## Example Snippets

### Good Opening Paragraph
"The Gmail Connection lets TenderFlow automatically read your tender alert emails and add them to your Inbox. Instead of manually copying information from emails, the system does it for you - saving hours each week."

### Bad Opening Paragraph
"The Gmail Connection utilises OAuth 2.0 authentication protocol to establish a secure connection with Google's Gmail API, enabling automated email ingestion and parsing via machine learning algorithms."

### Good Troubleshooting Entry
**Problem**: Emails aren't syncing
**Symptoms**: You connected Gmail an hour ago but don't see any emails in your Inbox
**Solution**:
1. Check your connection status in Integrations - it should show "Connected" in green
2. Click "Sync Now" to force a manual sync
3. Check you're forwarding emails to the correct address
4. Look in your Gmail inbox - are tender emails actually arriving?
5. Still nothing? Contact support with your connection details

### Bad Troubleshooting Entry
**Error**: Sync failure
**Resolution**: Check logs and verify API credentials are valid. Ensure refresh token hasn't expired.

### Good Best Practice Tip
💡 **Set up email forwarding**: Create a Gmail filter that automatically forwards all tender alerts from your local councils to TenderFlow. This means you'll never miss an opportunity, even if you're on holiday.

### Bad Best Practice Tip
💡 **Optimise sync frequency**: Configure polling intervals based on expected email volume to balance real-time updates with API rate limits.
```

## Template Variables

When writing tutorials, replace these variables:

- `{{feature_name}}` - Name of the feature (e.g., "Gmail Connection")
- `{{category}}` - Getting Started, Core Features, Integrations, or Advanced
- `{{time_to_complete}}` - Estimated setup time (e.g., "15 minutes")
- `{{prerequisites}}` - What they need before starting
- `{{related_features}}` - Links to related tutorials
